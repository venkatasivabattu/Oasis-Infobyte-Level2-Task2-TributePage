S.P.Bala Subramanyam

S.P.Bala subramanyam is popularly known as SPB or SP Balu or Balu, was an Indian playback singer, television presenter, actor, music composer, dubbing artist, and film producer. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest Indian singers of all time.

Early Life

☛ S. P. Balasubrahmanyam was born into a Telugu Brahmin family in Nellore, Andhra Pradesh.
☛ His father, S. P. Sambamurthy, was a Harikatha artist, and his mother was Sakunthalamma.
☛ Balasubrahmanyam developed an interest in music at an early age and studied musical notations.
☛ Despite initially intending to become an engineer, he continued to pursue music and won awards in singing competitions.
☛ He became the leader of a light music troupe and gained recognition as a talented singer, impressing renowned judges like S. P. Kodandapani and Ghantasala.

Musical Career

☛ Balasubrahmanyam made his debut as a playback singer in 1966 with the Telugu film 'Sri Sri Sri Maryada Ramanna,' and his first non-Telugu song was in Kannada for the film 'Nakkare Ade Swarga' in the same year.
☛ He gained recognition in the Telugu cinema with the song "Medante Meda Kaadu" from the film 'Sukha Dukhalu' in 1968.
☛ Balasubrahmanyam collaborated with renowned composers like M. S. Viswanathan, Ilaiyaraaja, and A. R. Rahman and recorded numerous hit songs in Tamil and other South Indian languages.
☛ He received his first National Film Award for Best Male Playback Singer for his work in the Telugu film 'Sankarabharanam' in 1980 and won another National Film Award for his Hindi debut in 'Ek Duuje Ke Liye' in 1981.
☛ Balasubrahmanyam's association with Ilaiyaraaja resulted in successful collaborations on films like 'Saagara Sangamam' and 'Rudraveena,' for which they both won National Awards.

Personal Life

☛ Balasubrahmanyam married Savithri in 1969 and they have two children, Pallavi and S. P. Charan.
☛ He considered S. P. Kodandapani, who gave him his first singing opportunity and mentored him, to be his guru.
☛ Balasubrahmanyam often paid tributes to Kodandapani and named his recording theatre after him.
☛ He also named his film production company as 'Sri Kodandapani Film Circuits' in honor of his mentor.
☛ Balasubrahmanyam held a deep respect and admiration for Kodandapani throughout his life.

Last Days

☛ On August 5, 2020, Balasubrahmanyam tested positive for COVID-19 and was admitted to MGM Healthcare in Chennai.
☛ His health deteriorated, and he was moved to the intensive care unit in critical condition, requiring a ventilator and ECMO support.
☛ Due to his age (74 years) and underlying comorbidities like diabetes, his condition worsened.
☛ During his hospitalization, his son Charan provided updates on social media, and the film industry organized prayers and vigils for his recovery.
☛ Balasubrahmanyam passed away on September 25, 2020, at 1:04 pm (IST) due to cardio-respiratory arrest. He was buried at his farm house in Thiruvallur district with state honors.
